About

Leveraging vehicle crash data from the United States, this web application will provide the likelihood of injury should someone get in an auto accident.

Background
The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) is a federal agency that aims to minimize vehicular incidents and potential harm from the incidents. The NHTSA publishes multi-year datasets with a multitude of information ranging from age of the driver to weather conditions during an incident.
Data
Data was downloaded from the NHTSA Crash Reporting Sampling System (CRSS) website. The data was a random sampling from millions of reported crashes each year, each dataset had over 100,000 accidents. All Gas No Brakes focused on three datasets pertaining to Accidents, Vehicles, and People.

About All Gas No Brakes
All Gas No Brakes is a composed of five students from the University of Minnesota Data Analytics and Visualizations course. We created this website in an effort to let users interact with our interface and leverage the machine learning model we created to predict severity of an injury in the result of a crash.
